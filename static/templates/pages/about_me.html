A little about myself

My name is Jon Tedesco, and I am graduate student studying computer science at the University of Illinois at Urbana Champaign, pursuing the accelerated master's program at the University of Illinois.

I have a passion for technology, research, and excellence, and when I am not working on school projects or spending time on the job, I develop personal projects, explore new technologies, and stay involved on campus through professional organizations and musical activities.

As I approach the next chapter of my career, I look forward to confronting interesting challenges in the field of software engineering, and seek a company in need of competitive technical skills problem solving ability, adaptability, determination, and creativity.

course work

What I'm learning this semester


CS 412 · Data Mining
Concepts, techniques, and systems of data warehousing and data mining. Design and implementation of data warehouse and on-line analytical processing (OLAP) systems; data mining concepts, methods, systems, implementations, and applications.

CS598 · Machine Learning for Signal Processing
Today we see an increasing need for machines that can understand complex real-world signals, such as speech, images, movies, music, biological and mechanical readings, etc. In this course we will cover the fundamentals of machine learning and signal processing as they pertain to this goal, as well as exciting recent developments. This class covers how to decompose, analyze, classify, detect and consolidate signals, and examine various commonplace operations such as finding faces from camera feeds, organizing personal music collections, designing speech dialog systems and understanding movie content.

CS597 · Thesis Study
Independent work towards master's thesis in graph mining.

current work

Current extracurricular work

Software Developer for CoMoTo

Since Fall 2010, I have helped develop software for the CoMoTo project, or Collaboration Monitoring Toolkit. This software uses advanced software similarity analysis tools to help instructors detect cheating in several mainstream undergraduate classes. However, our team likewise uses the tool to study how students collaborate and how students learn in these classes.



Programming Studio Moderator

Since Spring 2011, I have been acting as an undergraduate teaching assistant for the undergraduate programming studio course, CS 242, through which I help students with substantive software development projects and real-world programming practices.

extracurricular activities

How I spend my free time


P.U.R.E.

For the past year, I have actively participated in P.U.R.E., or Promoting Undergraduate Research in Engineering, as the liason for the computer science department. We strive to involve undergraduates, particularly underclassmen, in undergraduate research by acting as an intermediary between undergraduate students, and graduate students and faculty, placing new researchers into active research projects each semester. Many of these students continue long term research with their mentors, and we believe this experience is a critical component of an undergraduate education.


Band

Each semester, I spend my time in several musical ensembles on campus playing the saxophone. During my time here, I have participated regularly in concert ensembles, the Marching Illini, the UIUC basketball band, and jazz ensembles.
