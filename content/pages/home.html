Current extracurricular work

Software Developer for CoMoTo Project
Since Fall 2010, I have helped develop software for the CoMoTo project, or Collaboration Monitoring Toolkit. This software uses advanced software similarity analysis tools to help instructors detect cheating in several mainstream undergraduate classes. However, our team likewise uses the tool to study how students collaborate and how students learn in these classes.

extracurricular activities

How I spend my free time

P.U.R.E.
In the last two semester, I have actively participated in P.U.R.E., or Promoting Undergraduate Research in Engineering, as the liason for the computer science department. We strive to involve undergraduates, particularly underclassmen, in undergraduate research by acting as an intermediary between undergraduate students, and graduate students and faculty, placing new researchers into active research projects each semester. Many of these students continue long term research with their mentors, and we believe this experience is a critical component of an undergraduate education.
